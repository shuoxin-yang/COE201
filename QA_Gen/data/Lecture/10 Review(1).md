## 3. Complexity of Algorithms
* **The Growth of Functions:** Big-$O$, Big-$\Omega$, Big-$\Theta$ notation
* **Complexity Analysis:** Time/space complexity; three types of complexity analysis
* **Complexity of Problems:** $P, NP, NP\text{-complete}, NP\text{-hard}$

---

## 4. Number Theory
* **Divisibility and Modular Arithmetic**
* **Integer Representations and Algorithms:** Base-$b$ expansions; binary addition/multiplication/division, fast modular exponentiation
* **Primes and Greatest Common Divisors:** Euclidean algorithm, Bézout's Theorem and its corollaries
* **Congruences:** Solving linear congruences, the Chinese Remainder Theorem
* **Theorems:** Fermat's Little Theorem, Euler's Theorem
* **Applications of Modular Arithmetic**

---

## 5. Cryptography
* **Classical & Symmetric Cryptography:** Shift cipher, substitution cipher, one-time pad
* **Asymmetric Cryptography:** Primitive roots, Diffie-Hellman key exchange, MITM attacks; RSA encryption/decryption, RSA digital signature
* **Cryptographic Protocols:** Additive secret sharing, Shamir's threshold secret sharing

---

## 6. Induction and Recursion
* **Mathematical Induction:** Mathematical induction, strong induction
* **Recursion:** Towers of Hanoi, correctness proof, closed-form running time
* **Recurrence Relations:** Recurrence relations, iterating a recurrence
* **Divide-and-Conquer Recurrences:** Iterating D&C recurrences, Master Theorem

---

## 7. Counting
* **Counting Basics:** Sum / Product / Subtraction / Division rules, the Pigeonhole Principle
* **Permutations and Combinations:** Permutations, combinations, the birthday problem/attack
* **Binomial Coefficients:** Pascal's Identity, the Binomial Theorem, combinatorial proofs
* **Advanced Counting:** Inclusion-Exclusion Principle, counting onto functions; Solving linear recurrence relations, Generating Functions

---

## 8. Relations
* **Properties:** Reflexive, irreflexive, symmetric, antisymmetric, transitive relations
* **Operations:** Combining relations as sets, composite of relations, relation powers
* **n-ary Relations and Databases:** Primary/composite keys, selection/projection/join operators
* **Closures:** Relation closures, connectivity relation, the Floyd-Warshall algorithm
* **Equivalence Relations**
* **Partial Orderings:** Partial/total ordering, well-ordered induction, Hasse diagrams, lattices, topological sorting

---

## 9. Graphs
### Graphs I
* Terminology and Operations
* Special Types: Complete graphs ($K_n$), cycles ($C_n$), wheels ($W_n$), hypercubes ($Q_n$)
* Bipartite graphs, matching, Hall's Marriage Theorem
* Graph Representation and Isomorphism
* Connectivity: Paths, (strongly/weakly) connected graphs, cut vertices/edges, counting paths

### Graphs II
* Shortest-Path Problems: Weighted graphs, Dijkstra's algorithm
* Euler and Hamilton Paths: Euler circuits/paths, Hamilton circuits/paths
* Planar Graphs: Euler's Formula and its Corollaries, homeomorphic graphs, Kuratowski's Theorem
* Graph Coloring: Chromatic number, the Six-Color Theorem

---

## 10. Trees
* Introduction: (Rooted) trees, (balanced) $m$-ary trees, number of leaves
* Tree Traversal: Preorder, inorder, postorder; Binary expression trees (infix/prefix/postfix)
* Spanning Trees: Depth-first / Breath-first search
* Minimum Spanning Trees: Prim's algorithm, Kruskal's algorithm
